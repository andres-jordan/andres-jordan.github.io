My research has spanned a broad range of topics, concentrating currently on the discovery and characterization of transiting exoplanets, a topic on which I started working while I was a Clay Fellow at the CfA. I'm actively involved in HATSouth, a project that has built a global network of southern hemisphere automated wide-field telescopes to detect transiting exoplanets. I am the PI of CHAT, a 0.7m automated telescope currrently being installed at Las Campanas Observatory.
